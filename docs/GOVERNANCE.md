# Governance

## Publication boundary

Collectors may discover and normalize candidates automatically. Only reviewed, rights-cleared,
schema-valid records can enter a public snapshot. GitHub Actions should produce proposed diffs;
maintainer review publishes them.

## Source admission

A source is allowlisted only after review of:

- authoritative ownership and endpoint identity;
- API and data licenses or terms;
- commercial reuse and redistribution rights;
- attribution and notice requirements;
- schema stability, freshness, and deletion behavior;
- privacy impact and submitted-data consent;
- reproducibility and known bias;
- failure, suspension, and removal behavior.

## Record admission

Source approval does not approve every record. Each record must still have an exact artifact,
attributable evidence, compatible redistribution status, review expiry, and no prohibited personal
data.

## Independence

The project does not endorse a model or hardware vendor. Consumers decide what is safe, affordable,
licensed, and suitable for their use. Paid placement must never affect evidence or ranking; the
ledger does not publish a universal ranking.

## Disputes and removals

Corrections, provenance disputes, license concerns, and removal requests should be filed publicly
when possible. A maintainer may quarantine a record immediately while the issue is investigated.
Quarantine removes the record from future snapshots without rewriting prior immutable releases.
